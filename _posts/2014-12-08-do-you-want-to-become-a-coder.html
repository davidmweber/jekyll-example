What is the ‘X-factor’ that the codeX team is looking for?

A codeX coder needs determination, tenacity and grit, high levels of motivation and drive and a constant desire to learn more

We are looking for people who want to make things. That’s why when you apply to join codeX, we ask you to show us something you’ve already made.

If you go to the application form you will see a question which asks you to: “Tell us about something you’ve made, or something you’ve done to solve a problem. It need not have anything to do with technology. Anything you’ve done that shows your motivation. Why did you do it, what did it mean for you”.

So at codeX we’re looking for people with a very special set of attributes:

  • Do you have a problem-solving gene?
  • Do you keep trying to find an answer to things, or keep on twisting a problem around until you find a way through it?
  • Are you creative?
  • Do you think outside the box, bring a range of ideas from various experiences and different walks of life?
  • Do you think logically?
  • Can you take information from various sources and turn it into something that’s structured, in a way that a computer can understand it?
  • Do you have empathy for people?
  • Can you imagine yourself in somebody else’s shoes, can you imagine what the end user of software needs and wants?
  • Are you good at getting to the essence of a problem and finding ways to solve it? At codeX we work on real world projects for real businesses.
  • Do you work well in a team? Team-work is such an important part of coding.
  • Are you flexible? Are you eager to learn how to learn? We are not looking for ‘experts’, if you are already an expert coder, you should be out there coding already.

But if you fit the bill, and have that elusive “X Factor” we’re looking for, we have one last question for you:

“We’re building stuff for the real world in Africa. Why don’t you come join us?”
